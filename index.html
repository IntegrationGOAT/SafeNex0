Emulsification

An emulsifier (like Sodium Lauryl Sulfate) contains two parts:

Hydrophilic head – attracts water (polar side) Hydrophobic tail – attracts oil or fuel (non-polar side) When added to a mixture of water and oil (or fuel), the emulsifier molecules arrange themselves at the interface between the two liquids: The hydrophilic head faces the water. The hydrophobic tail embeds into the oil/fuel.

Scientific Basis and Reactions

Surfactant Chemistry: Emulsifiers like Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (C₁₂H₂₅SO₄Na) dissociate in water into ions that align at the oil-water boundary.

Saponification (Soap-based emulsifier formation): Fats or oils react with alkalis (like NaOH) to produce soap and glycerol — the soap acts as a natural emulsifier.

Services — How SafeNex Protects Lives

- SafeNex combines simple sensors, microcontroller logic and fluid mechanics to stop fires before they start. Below are the core services the system delivers when a crash occurs.

Impact Detection

A flame sensor detects sudden increase in temp with a radiating heat. The sensor sends an immediate signal to the controller

Decision & Trigger

The microcontroller (Arduino or equivalent) checks the sensor input and safety interlocks, then triggers the injection sequence in milliseconds.

Alert & Logging

The system logs all events and alerts the user via a connected app which is always monitored by the nearest police station, ambulance and fire station.

+ Acts proactively — neutralizes fuel before ignition is possible.

+ Minimal wiring and compact hardware suitable for retrofit.

+ Compatible with vehicle safety systems (can pair with airbag sensors).
